1.4 million fewer people received care for tuberculosis (TB) in 2020

An estimated 1.4 million fewer people received care for tuberculosis (TB) in 2020 than in 2019, according to preliminary data compiled by the World Health Organization (WHO) from over 80 countries- a reduction of 21% from 2019, as per WHO.The countries with the biggest relative gaps include Indonesia (42%), South Africa (41%), Philippines (37%) and India (25%).
