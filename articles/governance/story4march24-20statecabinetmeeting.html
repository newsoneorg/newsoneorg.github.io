MP cabinet approves amendment bill on unauthorised colonies

Penalty proposed, 10% of FAR limit to be increased to 20%

NEWSONE.ORG CORRESPONDENT
Bhopal, March 24, 2021

The state cabinet in Madhya Pradesh approved Madhya Pradesh Municipality Law (Amendment) Bill, 2021 in a bid to, as said the state government spokesperson, check unauthorised colonies in the state.

As per the state government spokesperson, ‘in the said bill, amendments have been proposed to clarify and simplify the process of registration of colonizers, the process of permitting colony development, so as to encourage the development of authorised colonies. A provision for strict action against violation of permission for development of the colony has been proposed’.

“Stringent provisions have also been made in the amendment bill to provide penalties for the offence of development of unauthorised colonies, in which it will be possible to curb unauthorised colony development. After the unauthorised colonies are identified, to ensure proper development of civil infrastructure in them and regularization of the layout of unauthorized colonies and to provide building permission in them, such provisions have been made in the amendment bill. Apart from this, a provision has been made to increase the present limit of 10% of FAR to 20% to reduce the crime of unauthorised construction”, said the state government spokesperson.

The decision on the amendment bill was taken in the state cabinet’s virtual meeting held at the state secretariat on Wednesday. The meeting was chaired by chief minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan.

As per another decision taken in the meeting, a sanction for Rs 316.96 crore was made for construction works related to expansion of specific medical facilities in Gandhi Medical College, Bhopal and allied hospitals with collaboration of the Prime Minister Jan Vikas programme of the Government of India. Also, the cabinet gave administrative approval for creation of 3 posts for establishment of viral research diagnostic lab in the Bundelkhand Medical College at Sagar.

Samman Nidhi to two journalists

Relaxing the state government’s rules the state cabinet approved a proposal for ‘Samman nidhi’ to two journalists Sunil Tiwari and Narendra Kulshrestha, as a special case under extraordinary circumstances considering the critical illness of the two journalists. They will get Rs 10000 per month each.

Approval for Bhind Sainik School

Keeping in view the health, safety and convenience of transportation in the industrial Area Malanpur for Sainik School, 50 acres of land has been identified by collector Bhind for allotment. The total expenditure of Rs 3 crore for the state government's obligations has been provided in the state budget. An amount of Rs 70 to 100 crore is expected to be spent for the construction of infrastructure, which will be borne by the Defence Research and Development Organisation. In this regard, the state cabinet gave its approval, as per the spokesperson.
