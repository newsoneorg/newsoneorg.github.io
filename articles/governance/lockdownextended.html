Covid surge continues, Lockdown extended from Friday evening to Monday morning

NEWSONE.ORG CORRESPONDENT

Bhopal, April 8, 2021

All cities of Madhya Pradesh will go under lockdown from 6 pm on Friday to 6 am on Monday every week effective from April 9 in view of alarming increase in Covid cases in the state. Till now, the lockdown was effective from Saturday evening to Monday morning.

Madhya Pradesh chief minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan announced on Thursday extending of the lockdown from one day to two days every week saying, “Measures like lockdown are the last option to prevent Corona infection. This is an unprecedented crisis.”, as per an official release.

CM said, “Necessary arrangements for treatment of patients and prevention of Corona infection are being made. In view of the alarming rise in Corona infection, it is necessary to impose a lockdown in all cities of the state from 6 pm Friday to 6 am Monday. Instructions have been given to all officers in this regard. Crisis management groups of all the districts have been instructed to hold meetings immediately to make necessary and appropriate decisions in view of the patient load and circumstances in their districts.”

CONTAINMENT AREAS

As per the CM’s announcement containment areas will also be made in big cities wherever it’s necessary.

CM said the state government was also constantly working on the expansion of the system of treatment. The number of beds in the state was being increased from 36000 to 100000. Covid care centers were being set up in each district. Cooperation of private hospitals along with government hospitals was also being sought. The state government was also arranging free treatment in private hospitals. Arrangements were being made in People’s Hospital and JK Hospital in Bhopal. Instructions regarding use of capacity of private hospitals in Indore and other cities too had been given.

OXYGEN SUPPLY

Amid reports from various cities including Bhopal regarding shortage of oxygen supply the CM said, “There is no dearth of oxygen supply. Discussion regarding oxygen has been held with the Government of India and state of Gujarat. The supply of oxygen from Bhilai Steel Plant has started. Procurement of Remdesivir injection has also begun at the government level. Adequate arrangements are being made to ensure that there is no shortage of medicines.”

“This is a pandemic any assessment of which at this juncture is not possible. The number of positive patients is increasing in the country and the state. It is necessary to maintain patience, tolerance and self-confidence. The state government will face this crisis along with everyone. A strategy for the battle against Corona will be developed by taking everyone into confidence. It is a testing time, we will fight together and succeed”, said the CM.

Chouhan appealed to people of the state to use masks while maintaining self-discipline, social distancing, washing hands frequently, not going out of the house unnecessarily and not forming crowds.

KAMAL NATH’S DIATRIBE

Hurling diatribe at the BJP government in general and chief minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan in particular in a series of tweets state Congress president Kamal Nath alleged, “There are no bed, medicine, injection and oxygen available in hospitals. There is atmosphere of fear in public. There are reports regarding deaths in Sagar and Khargone due to dearth of oxygen. On one hand people are suffering but on the other loot is going on.”

“Our chief minister must tell people how the 24-hour drama staged at Minto Hall benefitted the state and how it improved the health services. Today is the time to take decisions, alleviate sufferings of people and improve the health services in shambles.”
