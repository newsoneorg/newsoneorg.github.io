CAMPAIGN ON MASK

Chouhan to observe "swasthya agrah" to make appeal to follow Covid-19 protocols


Tankha blames politicians for spread for Corona

NEWSONE.ORG CORRESPONDENT
Bhopal, April 5, 2021

Madhya Pradesh chief minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan announced observing of a 24-hour "swasthya agrah" near Mahatma Gandhi statue at Minto Hall here at 12.30 pm on Tuesday, as per an official release.

The CM's announcement came at the end of his public awareness city tour, at Bairagarh on Monday while making an appeal to people from atop a vehicle to wear masks as one of most effective measures to prevent the disease.

In Bhopal, Indore and almost every district of Madhya Pradesh there are reports about most of people hardly observing Covid-19 protocols despite the second wave of Corona spreading across the state.

While starting the public awareness campaign on Monday Chouhan made his wife Sadhana Singh and sons Karthikeya and Kunal to wear masks at their residence.

Chouhan said he would hold discussions with medical experts, religious leaders, public representatives, social organisations, cabinet colleagues, media etc. on Corona through video conferencing. He will also hold a "Corona meeting" at the venue, as per the official release.

During the 24 hours period CM office will function under the open sky in Minto Hall while following the entire Corona protocol. There will also be a temporary NIC room for video conferencing.

Chief minister said, "A "Mein Corona volunteer hun" campaign is being started in the state from April 6 to ensure maximum public participation in eradicating Corona infection. Anyone can get himself registered as Corona volunteer by calling number 181 or on https://mp.mygov.in/ website. Corona volunteers will motivate people to apply mask, keep social distancing, get vaccinations etc. and will also help in this task."

He said no person should leave his house without wearing a mask. "If a person who is not wearing a mask talks to you, do not talk to that person. "Mask nahin to baat nahin", added Chouhan.

He also made an appeal to traders not to sell goods to such people who are not wearing masks.

Rajya Sabha member and senior Congress leader Vivek Tankha blamed the politicians in general and ruling party leaders in particular for being responsible for alarming spread of Corona in the country.

Tankha tweeted, "What kind of system this is! There are restrictions on crowd in marriages, funeral processions and temples but no restriction on politicians and political parties' rallies, crowds and road shows. A former chief election commissioner justified this in a TV channel programme. What has happened to educated and intellectuals of the country?"

Tankha said, "There is so much craving for power in ruling party leaders and despotic leaders that they are hardly bothered about people of the country. This country is running on God's mercy."

CENTRE RUSHES HIGH LEVEL PUBLIC HEALTH TEAMS

The central government has constituted 50 high level multi-disciplinary public health teams and deployed them to 50 districts across Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh and Punjab in view of the recent very large numbers of daily new COVID-19 cases and daily mortality being consistently reported by these states, said a Press Information Bureau (PIB) release on Monday.

The teams are being rushed to 30 districts of Maharashtra, 11 districts of Chhattisgarh and 9 districts of Punjab to assist the state health department and local authorities in COVID-19 surveillance, control and containment measures. 

"The two-member high level team consists of a clinician/epidemiologist and a public health expert. The teams will visit the states immediately and monitor the overall implementation of COVID19 management, especially in testing, including surveillance and containment operations; COVID appropriate behaviour and its enforcement, availability of hospital beds, sufficient logistics including ambulances, ventilators, medical oxygen etc., and COVID-19 vaccination progress", stated the release.

Meanwhile, the Centre has written to Delhi Government on alleged irregularities in Identification of COVID vaccination beneficiaries below the age of 45 years and issued show cause notices to private COVID vaccination centres seeking explanation, as per another release from PIB.
