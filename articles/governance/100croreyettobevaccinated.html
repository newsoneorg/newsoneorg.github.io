Fight against Covid-19

100 crore yet to be vaccinated against Corona, we need 200 crore doses: Pradhan

Chouhan cautions people against 3rd wave, emphasises on appropriate behaviour

NEWSONE.ORG CORRESPONDENT
newsone.org@gmail.com
Bhopal, June 12, 2021

Union minister for petroleum and natural gas & steel Dharmendra Pradhan said on Saturday about 100 crore people in India were yet to be vaccinated against Corona. Thus, India needed 200 crore doses of vaccines for which the Government of India, scientists and pharma companies were working constantly, said Pradhan as per an official release.

"One crore people will be vaccinated daily from June to mid-July. From June 21, a big vaccination campaign will be launched across the country", he said.

Madhya Pradesh chief minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan and the union minister Dharmendra Pradhan dedicated a 200-bed oxygenated temporary Covid hospital and laid the foundation stone of an oxygen bottling and refilling plant near Bharat Oman Refinery at Bina in Sagar on Saturday.

Addressing a gathering on the occasion Pradhan said that Madhya Pradesh had given a new model to the country, which could be adopted throughout the country. The infection was controlled by conducting door-to-door surveys. Corona vaccination was a panacea to prevent corona infection. Vaccination was mandatory for all, he added.

Congratulating CM Chouhan, Pradhan said, "The way in which the crisis management committee handled the situation in Madhya Pradesh is exemplary."

Pradhan informed the gathering about the CM's target is to vaccinate the entire people of Madhya Pradesh by March 2022. He also congratulated the officials of Bina Refinery and informed that there was no alternative arrangement for oxygen supply and refilling in Sagar. He said, "The construction of oxygen bottling and refilling station has been started here in collaboration with Bina Refinery. With this, a capacity of 25 tonnes of oxygen per day has been developed here. (As many as) 64 filling points have also been created here, through which about 2500 to 3000 big oxygen cylinders can be filled. Thus, along with Sagar, the availability of oxygen is ensured in other districts as well."

On the occasion Chouhan said, "We will leave no stone unturned for the possible third wave of Corona. For this, all arrangements are being made in advance. In this sequence, the hospital was dedicated in Bina."

Chouhan said, "Corona virus variant keeps changing. We all faced dangerous infection during the second wave. We must not ignore any possibility and be prepared for every situation in future. All necessary arrangements in this connection are being ensured. This hospital has been developed as a precautionary measure and we pray to God that no such need arises for this hospital."

He said, "Bottling and refilling plant is being constructed in collaboration with Bina Refinery, so that necessary oxygen will be available for the treatment of Corona infection. Also, oxygen cylinders can be sent to other places through refilling and Madhya Pradesh will be self-sufficient in terms of oxygen."

Chouhan said, "Corona infection is now under control in Madhya Pradesh. Today the state's Corona positivity rate is 0.46 percent. There are 24 districts of the state where not a single case of Corona has been reported today. As of today, there are three cases in Sagar and 15 in entire Bundelkhand but we should neither be reckless nor must we stop taking precautions. Rather, we must continue to adopt consistent Covid Appropriate Behaviour i.e. behaviour to prevent Covid infection, which includes wearing mask, using sanitizer or soap, not forming crowds and get vaccinated."

"This is the time to show restraint and patience. At present, no big events such as weddings, religious rituals and other programmes can be organized. Infection spreads only due to carelessness and laxity. Only restraint and precautions will come in handy in preventing infection. Media should also play its role in educating the public. Along with this, Corona warriors should also make the public aware", said the CM.
