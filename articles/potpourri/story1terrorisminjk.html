Civilian deaths increased in cross firing but decreased in terrorist violence in J&K

NEWSONE.ORG CORRESPONDENT
Bhopal, March 24, 2021

Number of civilian deaths increased in cross firing but decreased in terrorist violence in Jammu & Kashmir in the year 2020 in comparison to the same in 2019.

This information was furnished by the central government in a written reply in Rajya Sabha on Wednesday, as per a Press Information Bureau (PIB) release.

As per the information, there has been two civilian deaths reported in terrorist violence this year (up to March 15) but no civilian death n cross firing.

“There has been no civilian death in law & order incidents in J&K after abrogation of Article 370. However, due to terrorist violence supported from across the border, terrorists deliberately target civilians to put pressure on security forces. Despite the attempts of terrorists, the number of civilian deaths in terrorist violence and cross firing has decreased post-abrogation of Article 370”, stated Minister of state for Home Affairs G Kishan Reddy in the written reply.

“The next of kin of the civilians killed in the militancy related incidents, including incidents of cross firing between armed forces and terrorists, are paid an ex-gratia of Rs 1 lakh. Further, the family members of the deceased civilian have an option either to choose for appointment in government service or Rs 4 lakhs as cash compensation in lieu of the government service. In respect of the persons who sustain injuries in such incidents, ex-gratia as per the following scale is provided: Rs. 0.75 lakh for permanent disability; Rs 0.05 lakh for serious injury (hospitalization more than 24 hours); Rs 0.01 lakh for injury (hospitalization less than 24 hours) and Rs500 for minor injuries.”

In addition, under the MHA Scheme titled “Central Scheme for Assistance to Civilian Victims/Family of Victims of Terrorist/ Communal/LWE (left wing extremism) Violence and Cross Border Firing and Mine/IED Blast on Indian Territory”, a financial assistance of Rs. 5 lakhs is given to the family members in the event of death or permanent incapacitation of the victim as a result of terrorist, communal or LWE violence, cross border firing and mine/IED blasts on Indian Territory.
