COVID-19

MP & CG figure among 8 states with high daily new Covid-19 cases

NEWSONE.ORG CORRESPONDENT
Bhopal, March 29, 2021

Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh figured among 8 states in India that reported 84.5% of the daily new Covid-cases in the country in the past 24 hours, as per an official release.

The other six states include Maharashtra, Karnataka, Punjab, Gujarat, Kerala and Tamil Nadu. Madhya Pradesh shares its border with Maharashtra and Gujarat, hence the state government in Madhya Pradesh is alarmed over the situation and has announced a number of measures including Sunday lockdown in certain districts to check spread of the virus.

As per a report released by Press Information Bureau (PIB), Maharashtra has reported the highest daily new cases at 40414 followed by Karnataka with 3082 and Punjab with 2870 cases in the past 24 hours out of the total 68020 new Covid-19 cases reported from across the country.

291 deaths were reported in the last 24 hours. Seven states account for 81.79% of the new deaths. Maharashtra saw the maximum 108 casualties followed by Punjab with 69 deaths, Chhattisgarh 15, Karnataka and Kerala 12 each and Tamil Nadu and Madhya Pradesh 11 deaths each, as per the release.

Eighteen states/union territories (UTs) have higher deaths per million than the national average (117). Similarly, 18 states/UTs have lower deaths per million than the national average, as per the release.

On Monday, Madhya Pradesh reported 2323 new Covid cases with 9 deaths, as per the state government’s health bulletin released on Monday evening.

Alarmingly, as per the bulletin, it’s not only Indore and Bhopal which continue to report high number of new daily cases. While Indore reported the maximum number with 609 cases followed by Bhopal with 469, the districts like Jabalpur, Gwalior, Ratlam, Khargone, Ujjain, Betul, Barwani registered 159, 95, 94, 74, 72, 67 and 64 respectively. Also, Dhar and Narsinghpur 38 each, Sagar 35, Khandwa 34, Dewas 32, Mandsaur 31, Vidisha and Chhindwara 30 each and at least 16 districts reported new cases in double digits.

The PIB release states, “India’s total Active Caseload has reached 5,21,808 today. India’s present active caseload now stands at 4.33% of India’s total Positive Cases. A net incline of 35,498 cases recorded from the total active caseload in the last 24 hours.”

Five states, Maharashtra, Kerala, Punjab, Karnataka and Chhattisgarh cumulatively account for 80.17% of the total active cases in the country”, states the official release.

As far as recovery from the disease is concerned ‘India’s cumulative recoveries stand at 1,13,55,993 today. The national recovery rate is 94.32%. (Total) 32,231 recoveries were registered in the last 24 hours. Maharashtra has reported the maximum number of single day recoveries with 17,874 newly recovered cases’.

Fifteen states/UTs have not reported any Covid-19 deaths in the last 24 hours. These are Assam, Uttarakhand, Odisha, Puducherry, Ladakh (UT), D&D & D&N, Lakshadweep, Manipur, Tripura, Sikkim, Meghalaya, Mizoram, A&N Islands, Nagaland and Arunachal Pradesh, states the release.
