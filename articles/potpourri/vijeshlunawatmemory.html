SHIELDING PEOPLE AGAINST COVID-19

Vijesh Lunawat commemorated in vaccination of people against Covid-19

NEWSONE.ORG CORRESPONDENT
newsone.org@gmail.com
Bhopal, June 5, 2021

 

The deadly Coronavirus claimed his life hardly a month back within a couple of days of his hospitalisation. Post his demise former state BJP vice-president Vijesh Lunawat became a source of inspiration to shield others from the virus as hundreds took the vaccine free of cost at five places in Bhopal in commemoration programmes organised by his family on Saturday.

 

The BJP leaders and workers were led by their state president VD Sharma who reached Shivaji Nagar, one of the five vaccination venues, in paying homage to the late leader, inaugurating the vaccination camp and planting a sapling to commemorate the late leader.

 

Besides 115/49 Shivaji Nagar the vaccination camps were organised at his residence at 59, Vaishali Nagar; Digambar Mandir, Danish Kunj, Kolar Road, Adinath Shwetambar Jain Mandir, Tulsi Nagar and Lunawat Spiral CT Scan Centre, Motia Talab, all in Bhopal.

 

Late Vijesh Lunawat's elder brother and renowned radiologist Dr Shailesh Lunawat and his family members organised the camps, monitored the vaccination and motivated people to take the vaccine to shield themselves against Covid-19.

 

More than 1200 people were administered the vaccine at the five centres. June 5 being the World Environment Day those who took the vaccine were given saplings too for plantation. The beneficiaries include 20 students of a school for physically challenged. Tokens were distributed among the people who got themselves registered for the vaccination to ensure that those at the venues don't face hassles. All the necessary procedures were followed for the vaccination, said state BJP secretary Rajneesh Agrawal.

 

Besides the state BJP president VD Sharma the BJP leaders who were present at these venues include former BJP national vice-president Prabhat Jha, minister for medical education Vishwas Sarang, member of parliament from Guna KP Yadav, MLA Rameshwar Sharma, former minister Umashankar Gupta, former member of parliament Alok Sanjar, state BJP general secretary Bhagwandas Sabnani, state BJP secretary Rajneesh Agarwal, state BJP spokesperson Rahul Kothari, former MLA Dhruvnarayan Singh, senior BJP leaders Shailendra Sharma and Sunil Pandey, BJYM state secretary Ashwini Rai and BJP Bhopal district president Sumit Pachauri.

 

Others who were present include Dr CC Chaubal, Dr Akhilesh Jain, Deepak Goel, Mukul Lokhande and a number of doctors, journalists and members of other sections of society.

 

A close associate of late Lunawat and BJYM state secretary Ashwini Rai said, "There are people who help others during their lifetime but Vijesh Lunawat ji continues to inspire all of us to help others even after his demise. We will carry on the good work that he started for welfare of people. He always stood by anyone who was in need or crisis and who came to him to seek his help."

 

State BJP secretary Rajneesh Agrawal said, "We can't believe that Vijesh ji is not any more among us. He always inspired us to do our duties to the best of our capabilities to strengthen our party and help people. The vaccination camps organised today by late Vijesh ji's family are really true homage to the leader."

VIJESH LUNAWAT

Vijesh Lunawat began his political journey with Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad (ABVP). He held several posts in the state unit of the BJP including that of media in charge, spokesperson and chief spokesperson, general secretary and vice-president. Besides, he was an integral part of 'Jaoli', a group formed ahead of 2003 legislative assembly elections in Madhya Pradesh and led by Anil Madhav Dave, who is no more now, to chalk out strategies, coordinate among party leaders and monitor the BJP's election affairs across the state. After demise of Dave Lunawat headed the election management team of the party.
