Tributes

Chouhan visits Vijesh Lunawat’s residence, pays tributes, consoles family

NEWSONE.ORG CORRESPONDENT
newsone.org@gmail.com
Bhopal, June 12, 2021

Chief minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan visited former vice-president of BJP state unit Vijesh Lunawat's residence on Saturday to pay tributes to the late leader and console his family.

Vijesh Lunawat passed away on May 5 having been affected by Covid-19 after which condolences poured in from various sections of society. Those who pay tributes included chief minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan, union ministers Narendra Singh Tomar, Prahlad Patel, Dharmendra Pradhan and Thaverchand Gehlot, ministers in MP government, Rajya Sabha member Jyotiraditya Scindia, former BJP national vice-president Prabhat Jha, state BJP president VD Sharma, state Congress president Kamal Nath, former union minister Suresh Pachauri among others.

Chouhan paid floral tributes to late Lunawat at his residence and had talks with late Lunawat's wife Smita Lunawat and other family members on Saturday while recalling services of the late leader to the society in general and BJP in particular.

Earlier, Rajya Sabha member Jyotiraditya Scindia visited the residence of late Lunawat a couple of days back among other political leaders, according to BJYM state general secretary Ashiwni Rai who accompanied Chouhan and Scindia both.

VIJESH JI, IF POSSIBLE PLEASE DO COME BACK: CHOUHAN

Earlier, on demise of Vijesh Lunawat on May 5 Chouhan had tweeted, "I am shocked and saddened by the demise of former state BJP vice-president and dear friend of all of us- Shri Vijesh Lunavat. This is an irreparable loss for Madhya Pradesh BJP family. I pay my respect at his feet. My condolences to the bereaved family."

In a series of tweets Chouhan had said, "Our friend Shri Vijesh Lunawat was a dedicated public servant (Jansevak). He worked till his last breath to bring smile in the life of the weaker section of society and for progress of the state. He had immense dedication and commitment to his work. Today, we lost one of our true companions."

Chouhan had said, "Friend Vijesh Lunawat was a skilled organisation man, effective communicator and highly skilled in election management. He was a highly skilled strategist too. He had a great role in strengthening of the BJP in Madhya Pradesh. The vacuum created by his departure can never be filled. Vijesh ji, if possible pls do come back."

On June 5 Lunawat family led by Vijesh Lunawat's elder brothher renowned radiologist Dr Shailesh Lunawat organised vaccination camps at five places in Bhopal city in which more than 1200 people were administered free vaccine against Covid-19. Prominent BJP leaders including state BJP president VD Sharma were present on the occasion. The state president inaugurated the camp at Shivaji Nagar.

VIJESH LUNAWAT

Vijesh Lunawat began his political journey with Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad (ABVP). He held several posts in the state unit of the BJP including that of media in charge, spokesperson and chief spokesperson, general secretary and vice-president. Besides, he was an integral part of 'Jaoli', a group formed ahead of 2003 legislative assembly elections in Madhya Pradesh and led by Anil Madhav Dave, who is no more now, to chalk out strategies, coordinate among party leaders and monitor the BJP's election affairs across the state. After demise of Dave Lunawat headed the election management team of the party.
