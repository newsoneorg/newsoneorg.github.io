Amid ongoing turf war Kangana Ranaut gets best actress award

Marakkar-Arabikkadalinte-Simham bags best feature film award

NEWSONE.ORG CORRESPONDENT
Bhopal, March 22, 2021

Known for her strong and non-stereotyped roles in several films and currently at loggerheads with those whom she calls Bollywood mafia Kangana Ranaut won the best actress award as the 67th National Film Awards for the year were announced today. As per an official release from Press Information Bureau (PIB), the actress got the award for her roles in Manikarnika-The Queen of Jhansi and Panga.

The actress is presently enthusiastic about her role in Thalaivi, a biopic on Jayalalitha and MGR.

The best actor award was shared by Dhanush and Manoj Bajpayee.

The best feature film award went to Marakkar-Arabikkadalinte-Simham while An Engineered Dream won the best film award in Non-Feature Film category. Kastoori was awarded best children’s film award. Similarly, Shrikshetra- Ru-Sahijata was judged as the best arts and culture film.

Maharshi won best popular film providing wholesome entertainment while Anandi Gopal took the award for best film on social issues, stated the PIB release.

B Praak won the best male playback singer award for ‘Teri Mitti’ from the movie Kesari while Savani Ravindra won best female playback singer for her song Raan Petala from the Marathi movie Bardo. Gireesh Gangadharan has been awarded Best Cinematography for the Malayalam film Jallikkettu.

Sikkim won the award for Most Film Friendly State. Oththa Seruppu Size-7 bagged special Jury Award.
