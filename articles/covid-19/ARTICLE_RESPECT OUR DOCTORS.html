RESPECT OUR DOCTORS

Not all heroes wear capes, they wear scrubs too

Shailendra Shrivastava
(The writer is Retd IPS officer and ex-Director General/Chairman MP Police Housing Infrastructure Development Corporation)

RESPECT OUR DOCTORS: Only a life lived for others, is a life worthwhile - Albert Einstein.

This quote has never been as relevant as it is today, as we find ourselves in the midst of a pandemic that has cost us thousands of lives and a severe strain on our resources. In this scenario, it is extremely important to recognise the contribution and sacrifice of the men and women in scrubs who are risking their own lives to save ours.

When I was a child, my father always said that the best brains in the society would become doctors. So the best and most intelligent child in the family was told to pursue medicine. Further in life, as I joined the civil services, the civil surgeon was among the 4 most important authorities in a district, along with the collector, SP and DFO. Knowing this, it pains me deeply to see the mistreatment, abuse and disrespect that doctors face today, to such an extent that the government had to pass legislations specifically for their protection.

This, though particularly prevalent in rural Northern India where we still live in a "Dar (Thanedar and Tehsildar) Culture", can also be seen in urban India. The pandemic has reinforced the ideas my father instilled in me as a child, as we see doctors working under such difficult circumstances and against all odds, putting themselves and their families at risk. There is no greater service to the nation and to humanity.

Doctors save lives, but their contribution to society goes far beyond that. They ensure the well-being of a nation and functional productive citizens who can contribute to the progress of the nation. The pandemic has taught us that there is a need to re-evaluate our priorities and give doctors their due. We must protect their interests and do our part in supporting their efforts.

Not all heroes wear capes, and in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic, the real heroes wear scrubs.
